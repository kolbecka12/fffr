Our Partner Farms

The Farm Fresh Food Relief Program sources fresh produce from more than 20 farms in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Blue House Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Blue House Farm grows a diverse range of certified organic vegetables, fruits and cut flowers year-round, on 60 acres nestled at the end of a small agricultural valley in Pescadero, California. They specialize in strawberries, tomatoes, leafy greens and peppers for farmers' markets, wholesale purchasers, and their farmstand.

Brisa de Año Ranch

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Brisa de Año Ranch is a small organic farm that was founded in January 2018 by Cole Mazariegos, Verónica Mazariegos, and Cristóbal Cruz Hernandez. These three friends all work the farm and grow a variety of fruits, vegetables, and flowers along the beautiful coast.

Catalan Farms

Hollistar, CA  |  San Benito County

A third-generation farmer, Maria Catalan, is the first Latina migrant farm worker to own and operate an organic farm in California. Today she has nearly two decades of farming under her belt, and continues to grow organic, seasonal produce such as tomatoes, tomatillos, zucchini, chilies, and cucumbers on her Hollister farm.

Churchill-Brenneis Orchard

Ojai Valley, CA  |  Ventura County

Churchill Orchard began farming avocados in the Ojai Valley in the 1970s. With a focus on building healthy soil, they now farm 11 acres of tangerines and 3 acres of avocados and have been CCOF Certified Organic since 2007. Back in the 80s, Jim Churchill and Lisa Brenneis received crucial early support from Bill Fujimoto of Monterey Market in Berkeley who bought their entire production from 1989 – 1995 and slowly built a reputation for the Ojai Pixie Tangerine. Bill cultivates farmers with the same care that farmers cultivate their crops, and without him it’s possible that Ojai Pixies wouldn’t ever have found their market.

Earl's Organic Produce

San Francisco, CA

Earl's Organic Produce promotes organic agriculture and a sustainable food trade while cultivating enduring partnerships with growers and customers. They operate on the principles of innovation, education, and superior customer service to distribute premium certified organic fruits and vegetables to a diverse customer base.

Fifth Crow Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Fifth Crow Farm Grounded in a values-based approach to land stewardship, Fifth Crow Farm is a dynamic and diversified organic farm in Pescadero, CA. Founded in 2008 with a shoestring budget, a supportive local community, and ambitious dreams, Fifth Crow strives to bring eaters the highest quality, best tasting, and most nutritious food possible.

Frog Hollow Farm

Brentwood, CA  |  Contra Costa County

Frog Hollow Farm is a certified organic farm in Brentwood, CA that grows organically grown fruits that are sold nationally through their well-established CSA program, as well as farmers’ markets and restaurants.

Gallardo's Organic Farm

Salinas, CA  |  Monterrey County

Jose Gallardo comes from generations of farmers and grew up around agriculture his whole life. Jose is committed to growing organic because he believes “a child who eats a strawberry shouldn’t eat a spoon of chemicals, but just a strawberry.”

GGB Gardens

Walnut Creek, CA  |  Contra Costa County

GGB Gardens is run by Heather Anderson, a relatively new grower specializing in garlic, shallots, tomatoes, squash, peppers, and tomatillos. She is excited to support her community by donating fresh, organically grown produce to the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program.

J & J Ramos Farms

Hughson, CA  |  Stanislaus County

J & J Ramos Farms is an organic-practicing farm founded by the Ramos family in 1997. With cherries and apricots in the spring; peaches, nectarines and tomatoes in the summer; and citrus in the winter, their fresh fruit can be found at Bay Area farmers' markets throughout the year.

Pie Ranch

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Pie Ranch is a non-profit organization and farm dedicated to cultivating a healthy and just food system from seed to table through food education, farmer training, and regional partnerships. They normally sell their produce at their farm stand.

Ponce Produce

Watsonville, CA  |  Santa Cruz County

Founded in 2012 by Ruben Ponce who grows a wide variety of vegetables used in Hispanic cooking including hot peppers, Roma tomatoes, winter, and summer squashes, cilantro, beans, tomatillos, nopales, and chayote. Ruben has over 20 years of farming experience and he loves being out in the fields.

Rodriguez Farms

Watsonville, CA  |  Santa Cruz County

At Rodriguez Farms was founded in 1991 with 3 acres of strawberries along the coast in Watsonville, CA. overlooking the ocean. Since 2004, they have been CCOF and USDA certified organic, and today they now offer a wide variety of berries and vegetables. To grow their strawberries without pesticides, they use a special vacuum system to remove pests from the plants. Every year they carefully test the soil and make changes to ensure they are growing the best produce for Bay Area farmers' markets.

Sanzuma

San Rafael, CA  |  Marin County

Sanzuma is a small but mighty organic farm that was founded in April 2015 by Lori Davis the executive director of Sanzuma. Sanzuma is an all women-run farm. They grow a wide variety of fruits, vegetables, and flowers. Tucked behind San Pedro Elementary school in San Rafael the farm provides the San Rafael City schools with weekly produce deliveries, and CSA boxes to the community. The farm also practices beekeeping and vermiculture.

Smit Farms

Linden, CA  |  San Joaquin County

At Smit Farms, the Smit family has been growing top quality organic fruit, including apples, table grapes, cherries, stone fruit, blueberries, pomegranates and kiwifruits for over four decades. Smit Farms is committed to sustainable farming practices that will allow the soils and those tilling them to coexist in the most natural environment possible.

Veritable Vegetable

San Francisco, CA

Veritable Vegetable is a women-owned and led organic produce distribution company based in San Francisco, California since 1974. They source and deliver the freshest and highest quality produce, with unmatched service. Powered by a zero emission green fleet and driven by purpose, they put their values in action to build a sustainable and equitable food system.

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm a Bay Area Resident

How can I get a Farm Fresh Food Relief box for my family?

Every week, we deliver around 3000 Farm Fresh Food Relief boxes around the Bay Area. Although many of these boxes are delivered directly to the homes of food-insecure families, there are many boxes distributed to the public on a first-come, first-serve basis.

The current cities in which members of the public can find these boxes include: East Palo Alto, Pescadero, Brentwood and Stockton. For a current list of locations and times of these distributions, please reach out to us at programs@freshapproach.org

In the coming weeks and months we hope to add more public distribution sites. Stay tuned!

How can I find low cost / free food resources in my city?

Visit bayareacommunity.org to find free food offerings at school meal sites, food pantries, medical and mental health services, legal consultation and crisis hotlines.

If you receive CalFresh EBT (SNAP) or WIC benefits, you are eligible to receive discounts at many Bay Area farmers' markets. Visit marketmatch.org to find a participating farmers' market near you.

How can I support the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program?

Thanks for your interest! The best way to help us expand our program is to make a donation. If you'd like to volunteer your time, contact one of our partner organizations in your area and ask how you can help.

I'm a Farmer or Community Partner

I represent a community organization. How can I get boxes for my community?

Due to the demand of this program, at this time we are unfortunately unable to distribute more boxes to community organizations. However, we always love hearing from fellow nonprofit and community organizations engaged in food access. We believe where there is mission alignment there is always the possibility for partnership. Feel free to reach out to us at programs@freshapproach.org.

I represent a farm. How can we participate in the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program?

We are always on the lookout for amazing new farm partners. If you want to learn more about the program and how you might fit in, please reach out to programs@freshapproach.org.

I'm a Donor or Foundation

How can I help support the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program?

Thanks for your interest! The best way to help us expand our program is to make a donation. We are actively seeking partnerships with donors and funders in order to expand our impact and reach new vulnerable residents. If you represent a foundation, contact us directly at grants@freshapproach.org.

What is Fresh Approach's role in the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program?

The Farm Fresh Food Relief Emergency Food Hub is a highly collaborative project involving many moving parts in seven counties. Fresh Approach provides overall coordination, marketing and communications assistance, and technical assistance for the many organizations supporting this project. Donations are collected and administered by Fresh Approach, and will be distributed across partners in the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program.

How is the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program impacting Bay Area communities?

We are currently delivering around 3000 Farm Fresh Food Relief boxes throughout the Bay Area every week. The program is also providing a critical source of income to over 20 small, climate-smart California farms.
