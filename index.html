Our Partner Farms

The Farm Fresh Food Relief Program sources fresh produce from more than 20 farms in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Blue House Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Blue House Farm grows a diverse range of certified organic vegetables, fruits and cut flowers year-round, on 60 acres nestled at the end of a small agricultural valley in Pescadero, California. They specialize in strawberries, tomatoes, leafy greens and peppers for farmers' markets, wholesale purchasers, and their farmstand.

Brisa de Año Ranch

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Brisa de Año Ranch is a small organic farm that was founded in January 2018 by Cole Mazariegos, Verónica Mazariegos, and Cristóbal Cruz Hernandez. These three friends all work the farm and grow a variety of fruits, vegetables, and flowers along the beautiful coast.

Catalan Farms

Hollistar, CA  |  San Benito County

A third-generation farmer, Maria Catalan, is the first Latina migrant farm worker to own and operate an organic farm in California. Today she has nearly two decades of farming under her belt, and continues to grow organic, seasonal produce such as tomatoes, tomatillos, zucchini, chilies, and cucumbers on her Hollister farm.

Fifth Crow Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Fifth Crow Farm Grounded in a values-based approach to land stewardship, Fifth Crow Farm is a dynamic and diversified organic farm in Pescadero, CA. Founded in 2008 with a shoestring budget, a supportive local community, and ambitious dreams, Fifth Crow strives to bring eaters the highest quality, best tasting, and most nutritious food possible.

Fly Girl Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Fly Girl Farm, a four-acre women-run farm in Pescadero, CA, started in 2010 with a goal to improve the health of communities in the Bay Area. The farm now grows peppers, tomatoes, leafy greens, potatoes, cucumbers, strawberries, and more, plus vibrant cut flowers, all sold at farmers markets and to local chefs. Airielle and Kaeleigh, the farm’s owners, also actively mentor aspiring female farmers.

Gallardo's Organic Farm

Salinas, CA  |  Monterrey County

Jose Gallardo comes from generations of farmers and grew up around agriculture his whole life. Jose is committed to growing organic because he believes “a child who eats a strawberry shouldn’t eat a spoon of chemicals, but just a strawberry.”

Green Oaks Creek Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Green Oaks Creek Farm is a small certified organic farm nestled against the Santa Cruz mountains. The farm specializes in organic vegetables, strawberries, blackberries, herbs, flowers, and chickens for farmers' markets, local restaurants, and weddings that are held at their private venue onsite.

Lady Bug Farm

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

The UC Gill Tract Community Farm is a non-profit organization and urban farm in Albany, CA established in collaboration with the community and UC Berkeley's College of Natural Resources. Its mission is to conduct community-driven research and education, while fostering equity, health, and community resiliance.

Pie Ranch

Pescadero, CA  |  San Mateo County

Pie Ranch is a non-profit organization and farm dedicated to cultivating a healthy and just food system from seed to table through food education, farmer training, and regional partnerships. They normally sell their produce at their farm stand.

Ponce Produce

Watsonville, CA  |  Santa Cruz County

Founded in 2012 by Ruben Ponce who grows a wide variety of vegetables used in Hispanic cooking including hot peppers, Roma tomatoes, winter, and summer squashes, cilantro, beans, tomatillos, nopales, and chayote. Ruben has over 20 years of farming experience and he loves being out in the fields.

Contact Us

To Receive a Farm Fresh Food Relief Box for Your Family:

Boxes are distributed at partner organizations and food pantries throughout the Bay Area. To learn how to get free weekly produce boxes, please contact a nearby organization in your county of residence.

If you are a Community Organization, Farm, or Foundation:

Please send your inquiries to programs@freshapproach.org.

For All Other Comments and Questions:

Please submit your comments using the contact form blow.

If you have questions about a Farm Fresh Food Relief box you received, please state where and when you picked up the box in your message. If possible, please also include the tracking code from the sticker on your box.
