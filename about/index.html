About the Farm Fresh Food Relief Program

The COVID-19 pandemic has led to an unprecedented number of Americans experiencing food insecurity.

Today, an estimated 1 in 5 U.S. households don’t have enough to eat, and with unemployment approaching 20%, families everywhere are struggling to afford food.

Food banks have been overloaded with requests, yet California farmers have seen a dramatic drop in orders due to the shuttering of restaurants during the pandemic.

The Farm Fresh Food Relief (FFFR) Emergency Food Hub was created to get millions of pounds of unsold produce to the families who need it most.

By connecting small farms in Northern California through a network of community partners, we’ve found a way to get fresh, locally grown produce to the Bay Area’s most vulnerable households.

The more local farms we connect with, the greater the impact we can have — ensuring that no family goes hungry during this time of global crisis.

The FFFR Emergency Food Hub is addressing widespread food insecurity by distributing free boxes of locally grown, fresh produce to low-income families at community organizations throughout the San Francisco Bay Area.

In addition to delivering much-needed nutritious food to vulnerable Bay Area families, the FFFR Emergency Food Hub provides critical economic support to small farmers in Northern California, ensuring that the local food system remains intact and financially viable throughout the pandemic.

How it Works

1

Produce is grown at multiple farms throughout Northern California, and collected at a single partner farm for packing.

2

Partner farms pre-pack produce boxes on site with the most current CDC safety measures.

3

Boxes are distributed for free to low-income families at food pantries, schools, and other community centers throughout the Bay Area.

Our Partners

Boxes are distributed at more than two dozen partner organizations and food pantries throughout the Bay Area. To learn how to get a free produce boxes and whether you qualify, please contact a nearby organization in your county of residence.

Agricultural Institute of Marin (AIM)

Alameda, Marin, San Francisco Counties

The Agriculture Institute of Marin (AIM) is a non-profit organization that operates eight Certified Farmers Markets across the Bay Area. AIM's mission is to educate the public about the benefits of eating locally grown food, while connecting communities with the regional food system.

Edible Schoolyard Project

San Joaquin County

The Edible Schoolyard Project is a one-acre urban non-profit farm in Berkeley, CA dedicated to transforming the health of children by designing hands-on educational experiences in the garden, kitchen, and cafeteria that connect children to food, nature, and to each other.

Fresh Approach

San Francisco Bay Area

Fresh Approach is a non-profit with a mission to make healthy food more accessible in the Bay Area. Though farmers’ markets, nutrition classes, and community gardening programs, Fresh Approach works to empower people of all ages to grow, cook, and access more healthy, fresh produce.

Frog Hollow Farm

Contra Costa County

Frog Hollow Farm is a certified organic farm in Brentwood, CA that grows organically grown fruits that are sold nationally through their well-established CSA program, as well as farmers’ markets and restaurants.

Pie Ranch

San Mateo & Santa Clara Counties

Pie Ranch is a non-profit organization and farm in Pescadero, CA dedicated to cultivating a healthy and just food system from seed to table through food education, farmer training, and regional partnerships. They normally sell their produce at their farm stand.

Sanzuma

Marin County

Sanzuma is a non-profit organization and urban farm in San Rafael, CA dedicated to increasing the availability of freshly grown foods through gardening and urban farming, improving health, and reconnecting people with their environment. They normally sell their produce at local schools and restaurants.

Spade & Plow Organics

Santa Clara County

Spade & Plow Organics is a small certified organic family farm located in the South Bay Area, San Martin, CA with the mission of bringing back the agricultural legacy of "The Valley of The Heart's Delight”. They normally sell their produce at six South Bay Farmers’ Markets, year-round CSA programs, produce retailers, and restaurants.

UC Gill Tract Community Farm

Alameda and Contra Costa County

The UC Gill Tract Community Farm is a non-profit organization and urban farm in Albany, CA established in collaboration with the community and UC Berkeley's College of Natural Resources. Its mission is to conduct community-driven research and education, while fostering equity, health, and community resiliance.

Veggielution

Santa Clara County

Veggielution is a non-profit organization and urban farm in San Jose, CA dedicated to connecting people from diverse backgrounds through food and farming to build community in East San Jose. The farm sells its produce at a farm stand that is currently closed due to the shelter in place.
