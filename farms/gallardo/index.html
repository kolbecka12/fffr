Gallardo's Organic Farm

Gallardo's Organic Farm

By Lizette Calderon & Raffaella Cerruti  |  June 9, 2020

"The most beautiful part of it all is watching people take your produce home."

“The smile on people's faces and their happiness is the best form of payment for a farmer,” says Jose Gallardo, the owner of Gallardo’s Organic Farm located in the Salinas Valley.

He currently sells his produce at several farmers markets all throughout Northern California, including the South Lake Tahoe Farmers Market, the East Palo Alto Community Farmers’ Market, the Portola Valley Farmers’ Market, and San Carlos Farmers’ Market.

Jose comes from generations of farmers and grew up around agriculture his whole life. Although working a farm is hard work, Jose says it’s a “beautiful experience being able to work your land, plant the seeds, and watch your vegetables grow until you harvest them. The most beautiful part of it all is watching people take your produce home.”

Jose truly has a passion for what he does and is committed to selling organic because his father passed away from being exposed to many chemicals while he was working in a conventional strawberry farm in Mexico.

Just before the pandemic hit, Jose planned to move into a larger farm, where he could accomplish his vision to greatly advance production and sales. Then, the unexpected COVID-19 crisis unfolded.

Jose has never experienced anything like this in his lifetime. “At the beginning, we didn’t have anywhere to sell our vegetables, there were no open farmers’ markets, there were no ways to get our products out.”

During this difficult time, Jose had no choice but to throw the produce back into the dirt. Jose was then invited by Fresh Approach to start to supply produce for the new Farm Fresh Food Relief project.

Being part of this project has helped Jose to shift his uncertainties and fears for the future to a road forward and his prospects seemed finally to be looking up again.
