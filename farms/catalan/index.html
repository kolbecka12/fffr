Catalan Family Farm

Catalan Family Farm

By Isabel Medina & Andie Kolbeck  |  March 7, 2020

The spirit of farming runs deep in Maria's veins.

“A third generation farmer, Maria Catalan – founder of Catalan Farms in Salinas, CA – is proud to have taught her grandchildren how to preserve the environment like her grandfather taught her in Mexico.

In the 1960’s, her father and grandfather – both farmers from Guerrero, Mexico – were hired through the Bracero program, which brought millions of Mexican contract laborers to work on U.S. farms. Her whole family moved with them to Douglas, Texas, and she grew up playing in the cultivated fields, running and playing among endless fields of corn and peanuts.

When she got older, Maria grew interested in starting a small scale organic farm. Now living in California, Maria took an organic farming workshop in Salinas at the Rural Development Center.

Driven to follow her ancestor’s agricultural practice, Maria began attending organic farming conferences in the Bay Area. These were all conducted in English, so she’d stay up late studying the farming literature while simultaneously learning to read in English, unraveling the text by studying the photos.

Eventually, Maria went on to become the 1st recognized female farm laborer to become a farm owner in the U.S, and was the first Hispanic woman to to start a CSA (community supported agriculture) project in Salinas, CA. Today she has nearly two decades of farming under her belt, and continues to grow organic, seasonal produce such as tomatoes, tomatillos, zucchini, chilies, and cucumbers on her Salinas farm.

However, she still feels that creating a life for her children and grandchildren on the farm has been her greatest achievement. “My grandchildren all grew up on the ranch, eating plenty of good food, running along the tomatoes and eating strawberries from the vines. They grew up learning how to love the land and take care of her; they know how to take care of the air, the water, the dirt. For me that was my dream and I achieved it.”
